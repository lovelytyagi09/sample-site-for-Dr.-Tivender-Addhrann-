Vaccines by Age Group

Every age deserves the right protection at the right time. Dr. Tivender follows IAP-recommended schedules tailored to each child and adult.

0 – 6 Weeks

Newborn & Early Infant

The most critical window for immunization. At birth and in the first weeks, essential vaccines set the foundation for lifelong immunity.

BCG Hepatitis B OPV-0 DPT Hib IPV
6 – 24 Months

Infants & Toddlers

Key disease-preventing vaccines during the most vulnerable period of a child's development and rapid immune system growth.

Rotavirus Pneumococcal MMR Varicella Typhoid Hep A
2 – 6 Years

Pre-School Children

Booster doses and new vaccines ensure continued protection as children start interacting with peers in school and play environments.

DPT Booster OPV Booster MMR-2 Varicella-2 Flu Annual
7 – 18 Years

School-Age & Adolescents

Adolescence is a catch-up window. Key vaccines protect against serious diseases as children transition into young adults.

Tdap HPV Meningococcal Hep B (catch-up) Flu
18 – 60 Years

Adults

Immunity doesn't last forever. Adults need regular boosters and new vaccines to stay protected against evolving disease threats.

Flu (Annual) Td Booster Hep B Typhoid COVID-19 Pneumo
60+ Years

Senior Citizens

Immune systems weaken with age. Targeted vaccines for seniors can prevent life-threatening illnesses and hospitalizations.

Flu (Annual) Pneumococcal Shingles Tdap COVID Booster
