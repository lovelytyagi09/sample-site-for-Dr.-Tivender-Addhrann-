Why Checkups Matter

Prevention is Better Than Cure

Most serious diseases are silent in early stages. Regular health checkups catch risks before they become problems.

Early Disease Detection

Identify diabetes, hypertension, thyroid disorders and anaemia before symptoms appear — when they are easiest to treat.

Cardiovascular Risk Screening

Lipid profile, ECG, and blood pressure evaluation to assess your heart health and reduce the risk of a cardiac event.

Personalised Health Baseline

Know your numbers — blood sugar, cholesterol, kidney function — and track changes year-on-year with your personal health record.

Whole Family Coverage

From children to seniors — one clinic, one trusted doctor, complete health screening plans tailored to every age group.

Your Checkup Visit

What to Expect on the Day

Your health checkup at Trisha Family Clinic is unhurried, thorough and fully guided — from the moment you walk in until you have your results and a personalised health plan.

Schedule Your Visit
01
Registration & Pre-Consultation

Fill your health history form and share any existing reports. Our team reviews your concerns before you meet the doctor.

02
Clinical Examination by Dr. Tivender

Blood pressure, BMI, heart & lung auscultation, abdominal examination — a thorough head-to-toe clinical assessment.

03
Blood & Urine Sample Collection

Samples collected at the clinic with strict sterile protocols. Fasting samples (8–12 hours) are required for glucose and lipid tests.

04
Lab Reports & Imaging (Same Day)

Most routine reports are available same-day. ECG is interpreted immediately. X-ray reports within a few hours.

05
Report Review & Personalised Health Plan

Dr. Tivender explains every result in plain language and creates a personalised diet, lifestyle and follow-up plan tailored to you.

For Every Stage of Life

Who Should Get a Regular Checkup?

Children (2–17 yrs)

Annual growth monitoring, anaemia screening, vaccine review and developmental assessment.

Young Adults (18–35 yrs)

Baseline blood work, stress and lifestyle risk assessment, early metabolic disease detection.

Adults (35–55 yrs)

Cardiac risk, thyroid, diabetes, liver and kidney screening. Annual checkup strongly recommended.

Senior Citizens (55+)

Bone health, cardiac screening, chronic disease management and geriatric wellness assessment — every 6 months.
