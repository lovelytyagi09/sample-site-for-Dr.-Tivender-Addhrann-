Diabetes Mellitus

Managing Diabetes with Precision

Diabetes is a chronic metabolic condition affecting how your body processes blood sugar (glucose). Left unmanaged, it can damage the kidneys, eyes, nerves, and heart. Dr. Tivender provides complete Type 1, Type 2, and pre-diabetes care with individualised treatment plans.

Common Symptoms to Watch:

Frequent urination Excessive thirst Unexplained weight loss Fatigue & weakness Blurred vision Slow-healing wounds Tingling in feet/hands

What We Monitor:

HbA1c (3-monthly) Fasting Blood Sugar Postprandial Glucose Kidney Function (eGFR) Lipid Profile Foot Examination Eye Referral (annual)
Hypertension

Controlling Blood Pressure Effectively

Hypertension (high blood pressure) is often called the "silent killer" because it causes no obvious symptoms until serious complications — stroke, heart attack, kidney failure — occur. Consistent monitoring and treatment are vital.

Warning Signs (often absent — hence "silent"):

Severe headaches Dizziness or vertigo Nose bleeds Chest discomfort Shortness of breath Visual disturbances

Our Management Protocol:

Regular BP Monitoring 24-hr Ambulatory BP ECG Assessment Kidney Function Tests Salt & Fluid Guidance Medication Review Stress Management
Our Approach

How We Manage Your Condition

A structured, step-by-step care protocol ensures you receive consistent, evidence-based management that adapts to your body's response over time.

Did You Know?

Over 77 million Indians have Type 2 diabetes, and nearly 1 in 4 adults has hypertension. Both conditions are highly manageable with the right clinical partnership.

1
Initial Comprehensive Assessment

Detailed history, physical examination, and baseline investigations — blood sugar panel, HbA1c, lipid profile, kidney function, ECG, and blood pressure assessment across multiple readings.

2
Personalised Treatment Plan

An individualised clinical plan combining the right medications, dietary targets, exercise goals, and lifestyle modifications — tailored to your age, weight, co-morbidities, and lifestyle.

3
Regular Monitoring & Medication Review

Scheduled follow-ups every 3 months (or more frequently if unstable) to review blood sugar and BP readings, adjust medications, and screen for early complications.

4
Complication Prevention & Referral

Annual diabetic foot exams, kidney function monitoring, retinal screening referrals, and cardiac risk assessment to catch complications before they become serious.

5
Patient Education & Self-Management

We empower you to understand your condition — teaching glucose self-monitoring, recognising warning signs, and making confident lifestyle decisions between visits.

Common Questions About Diabetes & Hypertension

For Type 2 diabetics on oral medication, Dr. Tivender typically recommends checking fasting blood sugar 2–3 times per week and postprandial sugar once a week. Those on insulin should check daily or as advised. Your glucometer readings, combined with a quarterly HbA1c test, give the most complete picture of your control.

Type 2 diabetes can go into remission — especially in early stages — with significant weight loss, dietary changes, and exercise. "Reversal" means maintaining normal blood sugar without medication. However, this requires sustained lifestyle changes and regular monitoring. Dr. Tivender will assess your specific case and advise a realistic, achievable goal.

For most adults, a target below 130/80 mmHg is recommended. For diabetics or those with kidney disease, tighter control may be needed. For elderly patients, Dr. Tivender individualises targets to avoid the risks of over-lowering blood pressure, which can cause falls and dizziness.
